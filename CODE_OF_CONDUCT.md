# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We pledge to make participation in the CSC Sahayak community a respectful, inclusive, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of background, identity, or experience.

CSC Sahayak is an AI-powered ecosystem designed to assist Common Service Centre (CSC) operators by simplifying document processing, form automation, and citizen service delivery. We welcome contributors who are passionate about AI, public service technology, accessibility, and open-source collaboration.

## Our Standards

Examples of positive behavior include:

* Treating everyone with respect and professionalism.
* Providing constructive feedback and mentoring new contributors.
* Collaborating openly and sharing knowledge.
* Respecting different perspectives and technical approaches.
* Supporting the project and, if you find it useful, considering giving the repository a ⭐ star.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* Harassment, discrimination, or hateful conduct.
* Personal attacks, intimidation, or trolling.
* Publishing confidential or sensitive information without permission.
* Deliberately introducing malicious or insecure code.
* Any behavior that creates a hostile or unwelcoming environment.

## Responsible Development

As CSC Sahayak supports government-related workflows and AI-assisted document processing, contributors are expected to:

* Follow secure coding practices.
* Respect user privacy and protect sensitive information.
* Ensure AI-assisted features remain transparent and reliable.
* Thoroughly test changes before submitting a Pull Request.
* Maintain clear, well-documented, and maintainable code.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for enforcing community standards and may take appropriate action when necessary.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including GitHub repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions, documentation, and community communication channels.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainers through official project communication channels.
